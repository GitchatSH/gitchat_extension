# How we do outreach

Every once in a while we send cold emails to GitHub users we think might want what we're building. Here's the rules we hold ourselves to, so you can call us out if we break any.

## What we do

We read your public GitHub work first, and the email always mentions something specific from it. The sender is a real person from our team, with their full name and role. You get one email from us total, whether or not you reply.

## What we don't do

We don't send follow-ups, we don't buy email lists, and we don't share your contact info with anyone. After someone says STOP, we never email them again.

## How to make us stop

Three ways, all work:

- Reply `STOP` to any email. Record deleted within 24 hours.
- Click the unsubscribe link in any email. Same.
- Open an issue in this repo with label `outreach-complaint`. We respond within one business day.

## What we keep

Your email address and GitHub handle stay in a do-not-contact list forever, so we don't email you again. Anything else we noted (your repos, draft email) is deleted after 90 days.

If you want us to delete the do-not-contact entry too, ask and we will. Trade-off: we might accidentally email you again later, since you won't be on the do-not-contact list anymore.

## Why this file exists

People called us out for unsolicited outreach in [#198](https://github.com/GitchatSH/gitchat_extension/issues/198) and [#202](https://github.com/GitchatSH/gitchat_extension/issues/202). They were right. The first response we left was templated. This isn't.

If we break any of the rules above, we'll say so in the changelog below.
